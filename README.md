# HR Analytics Dashboard
## Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition, compensation, satisfaction, and workforce demographics using SQL Server and Power BI.
The objective of this project is to identify key factors influencing employee attrition and provide actionable business insights to improve employee retention.

---

## Tools & Technologies Used

- SQL Server
- SQL (SSMS)
- Power BI
- DAX
- Power Query
- Microsoft Excel

---

## Project Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition patterns.
- Identify departments and job roles with high attrition.
- Evaluate compensation trends across departments and job roles.
- Analyze employee satisfaction and work-life balance.
- Generate business insights to support HR decision-making.

---

## Dashboard Pages

### 1. Executive HR Dashboard

Features:
- Total Employees
- Attrition Count
- Attrition Rate %
- Average Age
- Average Monthly Income
- Attrition by Department
- Attrition by Job Role
- Attrition by Age Group
- Overtime vs Attrition

### Key Insights

- Overall employee attrition rate is 16.12%.
- Employees working overtime show significantly higher attrition.
- Sales Representatives experience the highest attrition.
- Research & Development records the highest attrition count.

---

### 2. Employee & Compensation Analysis Dashboard

Features:
- Average Monthly Income
- Average Years at Company
- Average Total Working Years
- Average Income by Job Role
- Job Satisfaction Distribution
- Work-Life Balance Distribution
- Education Field Distribution

### Key Insights

- Managers and Research Directors receive the highest compensation.
- Most employees report Job Satisfaction levels of 3 and 4.
- Employees generally maintain a balanced work-life environment.
- Life Sciences is the dominant education field.
